Benefits Questions, Answered

When can I add or remove employees from my group plan?

New hires, people leaving, a baby on the way: your roster changes all year, and the plan has rules for each situation. Here's when you can make changes without waiting for renewal.

Different changes follow different rules

Adding a new hire, removing someone who leaves, and changing an existing employee's coverage are three different things with three different timelines. Knowing which is which keeps you compliant and keeps your team covered.

The roster is never static, so the plan is built to handle changes throughout the year. Here's how each one works.

Adding a new hire

New employees can join once they clear any waiting period your plan sets, often the first of the month after a set number of days. They get a new-hire enrollment window to sign up, and missing it usually means waiting for open enrollment, so it's worth getting them enrolled on time.

Removing someone who leaves

When an employee leaves or drops below eligible hours, their coverage ends per the plan's rules, commonly at the end of that month. We handle the carrier paperwork, and we make sure departing employees get the required information about continuing coverage where it applies.

Changing an existing employee's coverage

Current employees generally can't change their elections whenever they want. They adjust during open enrollment, or after a qualifying life event like a marriage, a birth, or a loss of other coverage, which opens a short special window.

We track all of this with you so nothing slips, the new hire who needs to be added, the departure that needs to be processed, and the life event that opens a window. It's part of the year-round service, not just a once-a-year task.

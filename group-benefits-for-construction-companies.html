Benefits by Industry

Group benefits for construction companies.

Construction benefits have to fit a crew that's on its feet, around equipment, and often spread across job sites, all while you're protecting margins set at bid time. We help Minnesota construction and trades companies build coverage that holds up to the work and the numbers.

What construction companies need from a benefits plan

A construction team isn't sitting at desks. You've got field crews, real on-the-job injury risk, seasonal swings, and tight project margins. Benefits built for an office don't fit, so we design around how the work actually runs.

The work is physical, the crews move between sites, and not everyone is at a computer. That shapes the plan. Coverage has to account for injury risk, enrollment has to reach the field, and the cost has to stay in a range that doesn't eat your margin.

We're independent, so we shop Blue Cross Blue Shield of Minnesota, HealthPartners, Medica, UnitedHealthcare, and the regional carriers, then build a package that works for a crew, not a corporate office.

What we focus on for construction

Built for a physical workforce

Accident and supplemental coverage that pays cash for injuries, with the on-the-job scope checked so a field crew isn't stuck with an off-the-job-only plan.

Cost control on tight margins

Funding options and plan designs that keep the benefits line predictable when the job was bid months ago.

Enrollment that reaches the field

We run sessions that fit crew schedules and sit with workers who aren't at a desk, so the plan actually gets used.

Year-round advocacy

When an injury claim gets stuck or a worker needs help, your team calls us. We pick up.

Who we work with

Minnesota construction and trades companies from a couple of employees up to around two hundred, including electrical, plumbing, HVAC, and general contractors. Whether you're adding real benefits for the first time or replacing a plan that never fit a field crew, we can help.

How it works

  1. We learn your businessCrew size, seasonality, the kind of work, your budget, and where the current plan falls short.
  2. We shop the marketWe take your census to every major carrier and pull back real quotes with a few plan designs.
  3. We check the coverage scopeWe match accident and supplemental plans to a field workforce, on the job and off, not just one or the other.
  4. We handle the rolloutCarrier paperwork, enrollment that fits crew schedules, and the meeting with your team.
  5. We stay onClaims questions, injury follow-ups, and next year's renewal, which we start months ahead.

Beyond medical, we round out the package with dental, vision, life and disability, and supplemental coverage through Aflac, so your crew is covered for what a health plan alone tends to miss.

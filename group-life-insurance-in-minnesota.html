Income Protection for Minnesota Teams

Group life insurance for Minnesota businesses.

Group life is one of the lower-cost, higher-impact benefits a Minnesota employer can offer. It gives your employees' families a payout if the worst happens, and for a small business it's often the benefit that quietly says you look out for your people. We help you set it up the easy way.

How group life insurance works in Minnesota

Group life insurance is employer-offered coverage that pays a set benefit to an employee's family if they pass away. Most Minnesota plans start with a basic employer-paid policy and let employees add more on their own, all at group rates.

For what it costs, group life carries real weight. A basic policy protects your team's families, and it tends to run far less per employee than owners expect, which makes it one of the simplest ways to round out a package.

We shop life across multiple carriers, match the structure to your budget, and handle the setup so it's done right the first time. You decide how far to take it, from a straightforward employer-paid benefit to a fuller plan with voluntary buy-up.

What a Minnesota group life plan includes

Basic group life

Employer-paid coverage, often set at one or two times salary, and frequently available with no medical exam.

Voluntary life

Employees buy additional coverage for themselves, a spouse, or children at group rates.

AD&D coverage

An accidental death and dismemberment rider, usually bundled with the basic policy.

Portable options

Employees can often keep their voluntary coverage if they leave the company.

Who we work with

Minnesota employers who want to deepen a package with real financial protection, especially teams where employees are the primary earners at home. Whether you're adding life for the first time or revisiting an old policy, it's usually a quick, affordable win.

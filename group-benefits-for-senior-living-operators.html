Benefits by Industry

Group benefits for senior living operators.

Senior living runs on caregivers, and caregivers are hard to hire and harder to keep. Benefits are one of the few levers that move retention in this field, but they have to fit shift work, physical roles, and margins that don't leave much room. We help Minnesota operators make it work.

What senior living operators need from a benefits plan

Care staff work in shifts, on their feet, with real physical demands and high turnover across the industry. The plan has to help you keep people, reach a workforce that isn't at a desk, and protect a physical team, all while respecting tight operating margins.

Staffing is the whole game in senior living, and benefits are part of how you win it. But a plan built for an office misses the realities of a care floor, so we design around how the work actually runs.

We're independent, so we shop Blue Cross Blue Shield of Minnesota, HealthPartners, Medica, UnitedHealthcare, and the regional carriers, then build a package that fits caregivers and the budget at the same time.

What we focus on for operators

Coverage for a physical workforce

Accident and supplemental coverage that pays cash for injuries, with the on-the-job scope checked so a hands-on care team isn't stuck with an off-the-job-only plan.

Retention that holds staff

A plan that competes for caregivers in a tight labor market, where benefits can be the reason someone stays.

Enrollment across shifts

We run sessions that fit overnight and weekend schedules and sit with staff who aren't at a computer, so people actually enroll.

Cost control on tight margins

Funding options and voluntary lines that keep the benefits line sustainable in a thin-margin business.

Who we work with

Minnesota senior living and care operators from a couple of employees up to around two hundred, including assisted living, memory care, and home care teams. Whether you're building a first plan or fixing one that doesn't fit a caregiving workforce, we can help.

How it works

  1. We learn your operationStaffing, shifts, the physical demands, your budget, and where the current plan falls short.
  2. We shop the marketWe take your census to every major carrier and pull back real quotes with a few plan designs.
  3. We check the coverage scopeWe match accident and supplemental plans to a caregiving team, on the job and off.
  4. We handle the rolloutCarrier paperwork, enrollment that fits shift schedules, and the staff meetings.
  5. We stay onClaims questions, injury follow-ups, and next year's renewal, which we start months ahead.

Beyond medical, we round out the package with dental, vision, life and disability, and supplemental coverage through Aflac, so your team is covered for what a health plan alone tends to miss.

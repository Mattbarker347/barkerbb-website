How do I know if my plan complies with the ACA?

ACA compliance sounds intimidating, but for most small businesses it comes down to a few clear questions about your size and your plan. Here's how to read where you stand, in plain English.

It mostly comes down to size and coverage

Whether the Affordable Care Act requires anything of you depends first on your employee count, and then on what your plan covers and how it's reported. Most small employers have fewer obligations than they fear, but the details are worth confirming.

Compliance is less of a mystery once you break it into the pieces that actually apply to a small business. Here's the short version, and where we help.

Start with your size

If you have fewer than fifty full-time equivalent employees, you're generally not required to offer coverage at all. At fifty or more, you're an applicable large employer and are expected to offer affordable, minimum-value coverage to full-time staff or face a potential penalty. The full-time-equivalent math is where this gets technical.

Check what the plan covers

A compliant plan has to meet baseline standards, things like covering preventive care without cost sharing and not imposing lifetime or annual dollar limits on essential health benefits. Plans sold through carriers are generally built to meet these, but it's worth confirming, especially with any non-standard arrangement.

Don't forget reporting

Applicable large employers have annual reporting obligations to the IRS and employees. Missing those is a common and avoidable slip, and it's the kind of thing a broker keeps on your radar.

The honest answer to how you know you're compliant is that you confirm it deliberately, you don't assume. We check your size, your plan, and your reporting, and flag anything that needs attention before it becomes a problem.

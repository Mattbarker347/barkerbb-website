Benefits Questions, Answered

Can I offer different plans to different employees?

Maybe you want richer coverage for your full-time team, or a different setup for a remote group. The short answer is usually yes, you have more flexibility than you'd think, as long as you follow a few rules. Here's how it works.

Flexibility, inside some guardrails

You can generally offer different plans or different contribution levels to different groups of employees, as long as you define those groups consistently and don't design the plan to unfairly favor your highest earners. The key is using legitimate employee classes.

The freedom here is real, but it comes with structure. Here's what actually matters.

Group by legitimate classes

You can vary benefits by reasonable categories like full-time versus part-time, salaried versus hourly, or by location. What you can't do is single people out individually. The class has to be a real, consistent group, and everyone in the same class is treated the same way.

Watch the nondiscrimination rules

Benefits rules are designed to keep a plan from being built mainly to reward owners and highly compensated employees. As long as your classes are sensible and applied evenly, you're usually on solid ground, but it's worth a second set of eyes before you finalize the design.

ICHRA makes classes formal

If you want this kind of flexibility on purpose, an ICHRA is built around it. It lets you set different reimbursement amounts for clearly defined employee classes, which is a clean way to tailor benefits to different parts of your team.

We help you set the classes correctly, keep the design inside the rules, and make sure what you're offering holds up. That way the flexibility works for you instead of creating a problem later.

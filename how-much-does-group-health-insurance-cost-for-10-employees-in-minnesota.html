Benefits Questions, Answered

What does group health cost for 10 employees in Minnesota?

It's the first question almost every owner asks, and the honest answer is that there's no single sticker price. The cost for a 10-person team swings based on a handful of factors, and once you know them, the number makes a lot more sense.

Why there isn't one price for a 10-person plan

Two Minnesota companies with the same 10 employees can get very different quotes, because group health pricing is built around the people and the plan, not a flat per-business rate. A few factors do most of the work.

When someone asks what a plan costs, what they really need is to understand what moves the number. Here's what carriers actually price on, and where you have control.

The age and makeup of your team

Premiums are rated partly on employee ages, so a team in their fifties will price differently than one in their twenties. Where your people live in Minnesota factors in too.

The plan design you choose

This is the biggest lever you control. A higher deductible lowers the premium, a richer copay plan raises it, and the network you pick changes the math. We model a few designs side by side so you can see the trade-offs in real numbers.

How much you contribute

Your cost depends on how you split the premium with employees. Most employers cover a share and the team pays the rest through payroll. You set that split based on your budget and your goals for hiring.

How the plan is funded

A fully insured plan and a level-funded plan price differently, and for a healthy 10-person group, level funding sometimes comes back lower. It's worth quoting both.

The only number that means anything is a real quote built on your actual census, and that's free. We shop it across every major Minnesota carrier and bring back a short list with the costs laid out plainly.

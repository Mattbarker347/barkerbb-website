Voluntary Benefits

Supplemental Coverage that pays cash.

Accident, critical illness, cancer, and hospital indemnity coverage from Aflac and other carriers. When something happens, cash goes directly to your employee to spend however they need.

Fill the gaps your medical plan leaves behind

Even a great health plan has a deductible, a copay, and an out-of-pocket max. Supplemental coverage pays cash directly to your employee when something happens — an ER visit, a cancer diagnosis, a hospital stay — so they can cover the gaps without dipping into savings.

The best part for employers: these plans are typically 100% voluntary, meaning your employees pay the premium through payroll deduction. You can add this entire line of benefits at essentially zero cost to the business, while giving your team real financial protection.

What's included

Accident insurance

Lump-sum cash benefits for injuries, ER visits, fractures, and dislocations. Paid directly to the employee — use it however they want.

Critical illness

Cash payout on diagnosis of heart attack, stroke, cancer, and other covered conditions. Helps cover copays, childcare, travel, lost income.

Hospital indemnity

Pays a fixed cash amount per day an employee is admitted to the hospital. Stacks on top of their medical insurance.

Cancer coverage

Dedicated cancer plans that pay benefits for diagnosis, treatment, recovery, and ongoing care.

Who it's for

Any employer who wants to strengthen their benefits package without adding payroll cost. Supplemental works especially well for employers whose team is on a higher-deductible medical plan — where a big claim could mean a large out-of-pocket hit before insurance kicks in.

How we make it work

  1. Review your medical planWe look at where your medical coverage leaves gaps — deductibles, out-of-pocket max, specific exclusions.
  2. Pick the right productsWe recommend the supplemental plans that address the real gaps, not a bundle you'll never use.
  3. Set up enrollmentWe add them to your online enrollment platform so employees can sign up on their own time.
  4. Educate the teamWe run sessions that explain how supplemental actually works — because the benefits are only valuable if employees understand them.

Income Protection

Life & Disability for the unexpected.

Group life insurance protects your employees' families. Disability insurance protects their paycheck when they can't work. Both are high-impact, lower-cost additions that say "we've got you."

Protection your team hopes they never need

Nobody wants to think about not being able to work. But when something happens — an injury, an illness, a surgery — disability insurance replaces part of an employee's paycheck so their family isn't choosing between rent and recovery. Group life does the same for the unthinkable.

For employers, both of these are some of the lowest-cost, highest-impact benefits you can offer. A basic group life policy often runs pennies per employee per day. Voluntary options let your team buy up to coverage levels that actually match their family situation.

Group Life Insurance

Basic group life

Employer-paid coverage, typically 1–2x annual salary. Often available with no medical underwriting.

Voluntary life

Employees buy additional coverage for themselves, a spouse, or dependents at group rates.

AD&D coverage

Accidental death and dismemberment rider, usually bundled with basic group life.

Portable options

Employees can often keep voluntary life coverage when they leave the group.

Short-Term & Long-Term Disability

Short-term disability

Replaces a portion of income for temporary disabilities — surgeries, pregnancies, recoveries — usually for the first 3–6 months.

Long-term disability

Picks up where STD ends. Often the most overlooked benefit on the list, and the one families rely on most when something serious happens.

Who it's for

Any employer who wants to deepen a benefits package with real financial protection. Life and disability are especially powerful for businesses whose employees are the primary earners in their households — and they cost far less than most owners assume.

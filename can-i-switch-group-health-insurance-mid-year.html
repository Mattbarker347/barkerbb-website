Benefits Questions, Answered

Can I switch group health insurance mid-year?

You're not locked into a bad plan forever, but the timing rules are different for the employer and the employees. Here's how mid-year changes actually work, so you know what's possible and when.

It depends on who is making the change

There are two separate questions here. Can the business move to a different plan or carrier outside of renewal, and can an individual employee change their own coverage mid-year? The answers are different.

Group plans run on a 12-month cycle, but that doesn't mean nothing can change until it ends. The key is who is changing what.

The employer changing plans or carriers

As the employer, you generally can move to a different plan or carrier outside of your renewal date. Most companies still time changes to renewal because it's cleaner, but if your current plan really isn't working, you don't have to wait a full year. Timing and underwriting are worth reviewing before you move, and that's where we help.

An employee changing their own coverage

Employees usually can't change their elections whenever they want. They make choices during open enrollment, and after that they can only adjust following a qualifying life event.

What counts as a qualifying life event

Marriage or divorce, the birth or adoption of a child, a loss of other coverage, and similar changes open a short special window for an employee to add, drop, or change coverage.

If your plan isn't fitting, you don't have to white-knuckle it to renewal. We'll look at where you are, what switching would actually involve, and whether it makes sense to move now or line it up for your renewal.

Quick Facts

  • Employer switchGenerally allowed mid-year
  • Employee changesOpen enrollment or life event
  • Most common timingAt renewal
  • Qualifying eventsMarriage, birth, coverage loss

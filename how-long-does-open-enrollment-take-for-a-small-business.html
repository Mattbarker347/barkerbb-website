Benefits Questions, Answered

How long does open enrollment take for a small business?

The honest answer has two parts: the enrollment window your team actually gets is short, but the work that makes it go smoothly starts well before that. Here's a realistic timeline.

The window is short, the prep is what matters

For most small businesses, the employee open enrollment window runs about one to two weeks. The part that determines whether it goes smoothly is the prep that happens in the weeks before it opens, the quoting, the plan decisions, and getting the enrollment system ready.

When people ask how long open enrollment takes, they usually mean the window when employees pick their coverage. That's the short part. The longer part is everything that sets it up.

The employee enrollment window

Once the plan is set and the system is ready, employees usually get about one to two weeks to make their elections. Keeping it tight actually helps, since a longer window mostly just means people wait until the last day either way.

The prep before the window

This is where the real time goes, and it's our job. We shop the market, bring you a short list, finalize the plan and contributions, build it in the enrollment system, and prepare clear materials for your team. For a smooth renewal we like to start this months ahead, not weeks.

The enrollment meeting

For a small team, the kickoff meeting is usually an hour or less, in person or virtual, where we walk through the plan and answer questions. After that, employees enroll on their own time during the window, and we're available for the one-on-one questions.

The takeaway: budget a week or two for the window itself, but give the prep real runway. That's what makes the difference between a calm enrollment and a scramble, and the prep is the part we carry.

Options, Compared

Health Access Solutions vs traditional group medical.

These two cover a team in very different ways, and the choice really comes down to one tradeoff: what you spend overall versus what you're exposed to in a worst case. Here's the honest head-to-head.

The tradeoff is cost versus maximum exposure

A traditional group medical plan gives you a familiar structure with a defined maximum out-of-pocket, at a premium that tends to climb each year. Health Access Solutions is built to lower the total cost of coverage, with a maximum out-of-pocket that works differently. That tradeoff is the whole decision.

When we run this comparison for a Minnesota employer, we put it on the two numbers that actually decide it: the total cost to cover your team, and the maximum out-of-pocket exposure. Neither option is better in the abstract. It depends on which of those two you weight more.

How traditional group medical works

You pick a plan, your team enrolls at group rates, and the plan carries a defined deductible and maximum out-of-pocket. It's familiar, employees know how it works, and the worst-case exposure is clearly capped. The trade-off is the total cost, which tends to rise at renewal in ways you don't control.

How Health Access Solutions works

Health Access Solutions is a cooperative healthcare model built to bring down the total cost of covering a team. For many groups it's a clear win on that ongoing number. The trade-off shows up on the maximum out-of-pocket side, which is structured differently than a traditional plan, so it's something to weigh honestly rather than gloss over.

How to choose between them

Ask which matters more for your group: the lowest total cost, or the most predictable worst-case exposure. If holding down the ongoing cost is the priority and your team can work with the structure, Health Access Solutions often wins. If a tightly capped maximum out-of-pocket is what helps you sleep, the traditional plan may be worth the higher cost. For a lot of groups the savings tip it, and for some they don't.

We model both side by side on total cost and maximum out-of-pocket, with real numbers, so you're choosing on facts instead of a pitch. If the traditional plan is the better call for your team, that's what we'll tell you.

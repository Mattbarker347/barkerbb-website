Carriers, Compared

Delta Dental vs Beam for small business.

Both are solid group dental carriers we quote, and they tend to win on different things. One leads on network reach, the other on price and a simpler setup. Here's how to tell which fits your team.

Network reach, or price and simplicity

Delta Dental's strength is one of the largest dentist networks in the country, so most employees can keep their dentist. Beam's strength is competitive pricing and a clean, digital experience. The right pick depends on what your team values more.

We quote both as an independent broker, so this isn't about steering you anywhere. It's about matching the carrier to what matters most for your group.

Where Delta Dental wins

Network. Delta Dental has one of the widest dentist networks anywhere, which means the odds your employees can keep the dentist they already see are high. There's also real name recognition, which makes enrollment smoother. For a team that cares about keeping current dentists, that reach is hard to beat.

Where Beam wins

Price and simplicity. For a lot of small groups, Beam comes back competitive on premium, and the experience is built around modern software, so quoting, enrollment, and administration tend to be cleaner. For an owner who wants solid dental without the paperwork drag, that matters.

How to choose between them

Ask what your team would notice more: keeping their exact dentist, or a sharper price and a simpler setup. If network breadth is the priority, Delta Dental leans your way. If cost and ease lead your list, Beam is worth a hard look. We pull both quotes and put them side by side so the call is based on real numbers and real networks, not a name.

Either way, we handle the setup and stay on to service it, so your dental line runs without eating your time.

Benefits Questions, Answered

What's an SPD, and do I need one?

It's one of those benefits acronyms that's easy to ignore until it matters. An SPD is a required document that explains your plan to employees, and most employers offering group benefits need one. Here's the plain version.

The document that explains the plan to your team

An SPD, or Summary Plan Description, is the plain-language document that tells your employees what the plan covers, how it works, and what their rights are. Under federal law, known as ERISA, employers that offer a group health plan are generally required to provide one.

The SPD exists so employees can actually understand the benefits they have, in language that isn't a legal contract. It's a compliance requirement that doubles as a genuinely useful resource for your team.

What an SPD includes

It spells out who's eligible, what the plan covers, how to file a claim, how costs are shared, and the participants' rights under federal law. Think of it as the employee-facing user guide for the plan.

Do you need one

If you offer a group health plan, you almost certainly do. ERISA generally requires employers with these plans to give participants an SPD, and to provide it within set timeframes after someone enrolls or when the plan changes. There are narrow exceptions, but most small employers offering benefits are on the hook.

How to get it right

An SPD isn't just the carrier's benefit summary. It often needs a wrap document that pulls the required ERISA language together with the carrier materials. This is a common gap for small employers who assume the carrier booklet covers it. We help make sure the document exists, says what it needs to, and gets to your team on time.

It's the kind of back-office detail that's easy to miss and awkward to be caught without. Part of what we do is make sure it's handled.

Options, Compared

ICHRA vs traditional group health insurance.

Both get your team covered, but they work in opposite directions. A group plan picks one set of coverage for everyone; an ICHRA hands each employee a budget and lets them choose. Here's how to tell which one fits.

One plan for everyone, or a budget for each person

With traditional group health, you choose a plan and your whole team enrolls in it at group rates. With an ICHRA, you set a monthly dollar amount and reimburse employees tax-free for individual coverage they pick themselves. The difference is control versus choice.

This is one of the first forks we talk through with a Minnesota employer. Neither path is better in the abstract. It comes down to your budget, your team, and how much you want to be in the plan-picking business.

How traditional group health works

You select a plan, or a few options, and your employees enroll at group rates. You manage the relationship with one carrier, and the cost moves at renewal based on the group. It's familiar, employees know how it works, and it tends to fit teams that want one solid plan handled for them. The trade-offs are participation requirements and renewals that can climb in ways you don't control.

How an ICHRA works

Instead of buying one plan, you set a monthly allowance and reimburse employees tax-free for individual coverage they choose. Your cost is what you decide it is, which makes the budget predictable, and employees get to pick a plan that fits them. It works for any size and skips group participation rules. The trade-off is that employees shop for their own coverage, so they need a little guidance, which is part of what we handle.

How to choose between them

Think about predictability and choice. If you want one plan managed for you and your team values a familiar setup, traditional group leans your way. If you want a fixed budget and your people would rather choose their own coverage, an ICHRA is worth a hard look. Sometimes the group plan still wins, and that's the honest part.

We model both with real numbers so the comparison is concrete instead of theoretical, and we help your team enroll either way.

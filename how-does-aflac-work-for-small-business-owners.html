Benefits Questions, Answered

How does Aflac work for small business owners?

Aflac is one of the easiest benefits a small business can add, and one of the few an owner can usually take part in too. Here's how it actually works from the owner's seat, without the duck jokes.

Cash benefits, paid by payroll, light on the company

Aflac is voluntary supplemental coverage that pays cash directly to a person when a covered event happens, an injury, a serious illness, a hospital stay. Employees, and usually the owner, pay through payroll at group rates, so the business can offer it at little or no direct cost.

For an owner, Aflac solves two things at once: it gives the team meaningful protection, and it does it without adding a line to the company's budget. Here's the mechanics.

How it pays

When a covered event happens, Aflac pays a set cash benefit straight to the person, not to a doctor or hospital. They can spend it on the deductible, the rent, the drive to treatment, whatever the situation calls for. It pays on top of their regular medical coverage.

What it costs the business

Usually little or nothing. Because it's voluntary, employees who want it pay the premium through payroll deduction. You're providing access to group-rate coverage and a simple way to pay for it, not taking on the premium yourself.

Can the owner get it too

Generally yes. As an owner you can typically enroll alongside your team, which means the same cash-benefit protection applies to you, at those same group rates. For a lot of owners that's a quiet perk they didn't realize was on the table.

We set the whole thing up, handle enrollment, and help your people, owner included, file a claim when they need to. It's one of the simplest wins in a benefits package.

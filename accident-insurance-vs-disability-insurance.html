Coverage, Compared

Accident insurance vs disability insurance.

These two get grouped together because both come up after something goes wrong, but they solve different problems. One covers the costs of getting hurt; the other replaces the paycheck while someone recovers. Most teams benefit from understanding both.

Covering the injury, or covering the paycheck

Accident insurance pays a fixed cash benefit for the costs tied to an injury, the ER visit, the fracture, the follow-up. Disability insurance replaces a portion of an employee's income when they can't work, whether the cause is an injury or an illness. Different jobs, often a good pair.

When an employee gets hurt, there are really two financial hits: the immediate costs of treatment, and the income lost while they're out. Accident and disability coverage each handle one of those.

How accident insurance works

It pays set cash benefits for covered injuries and treatments, paid directly to the employee to spend however they need. It responds fast to the immediate costs of an accident, and it's usually voluntary, so the company can offer it at little or no direct cost. It does not replace lost wages over time.

How disability insurance works

It replaces a portion of an employee's paycheck when they can't work, and it covers illness as well as injury, not just accidents. Short-term disability handles the first stretch, often a few months, and long-term disability picks up from there for a serious situation. This is the coverage families lean on when someone is out for a while.

How to choose between them

It's less either-or than it looks. Accident insurance covers the sudden costs of getting hurt; disability insurance protects the income when someone can't work at all. A team with a higher-deductible medical plan often values accident coverage, while disability is the deeper safety net for a longer absence. Many packages include both because they cover different gaps.

We help you figure out which gaps matter most for your team and build the right mix, whether that's one of these, both, or a fuller package.

Benefits Questions, Answered

Do I need a Section 125 plan for pre-tax deductions?

If your employees pay part of their premium and you want those dollars taken out before taxes, there's a document behind that, and a lot of small employers don't realize they need it. Here's the plain version.

The document behind pre-tax premium deductions

A Section 125 plan, often called a cafeteria plan or a premium only plan, is what legally allows employees to pay their share of premiums with pre-tax dollars. If you're doing pre-tax payroll deductions for benefits, you generally need one in place.

This is one of those back-office pieces that quietly matters. The tax savings are real, for both you and your employees, but they depend on having the right document.

What a Section 125 plan does

It lets employees pay their portion of premiums before taxes are calculated, which lowers their taxable income and trims your payroll taxes too. The simplest version, a premium only plan, covers just the premium deductions. Fuller versions can include things like an FSA.

Why you generally need one

Pre-tax treatment isn't automatic. To take premium deductions pre-tax, the IRS expects a written Section 125 plan document to be in place. Running pre-tax deductions without one is a common gap, and it's the kind of thing that's easy to fix from here but awkward to be caught without.

The part employers miss

Plenty of small employers are already taking pre-tax deductions without the document actually on file, often assuming the carrier or payroll provider handled it. They frequently haven't. It's worth a quick check.

We help make sure the document exists and matches what your payroll is actually doing, so the tax savings hold up. It's a small step that protects a real benefit.

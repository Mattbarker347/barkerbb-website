Voluntary Benefits

Accident insurance for your employees.

Even a strong medical plan leaves a deductible and a pile of copays behind. Accident insurance pays cash straight to your employee when they get hurt, so a trip to the ER doesn't turn into a financial setback. For most Minnesota employers it adds to the package at little or no cost.

How accident insurance works

Accident insurance is voluntary coverage that pays a fixed cash benefit when an employee is injured, for things like ER visits, fractures, and dislocations. The money goes directly to the employee to spend however they need, on top of whatever their medical plan covers.

When someone on your team gets hurt, the medical bill is only part of the hit. There's the deductible, the time off, the drive to a specialist. Accident insurance pays a set cash amount for covered injuries and treatments, and that cash lands with the employee, not the doctor's office.

We set these plans up through Aflac and other carriers, almost always as a voluntary benefit paid through payroll, which means you can offer it at little or no direct cost to the business while giving your team a real safety net.

What accident insurance covers

Cash for injuries

Fixed benefits for ER visits, fractures, dislocations, burns, and the follow-up care that comes after.

Scope matched to your team

Some plans cover injuries on the job, some off the job, some both. We check that before recommending one, so a hands-on workforce isn't left with the wrong fit.

Stacks on your medical

It pays on top of the health plan, helping cover the deductible and the out-of-pocket costs a big claim leaves behind.

Voluntary and payroll deducted

Employees enroll and pay through payroll, so the company can add it at little or no cost.

Who it's for

Any Minnesota employer who wants to strengthen the package without adding payroll cost, and it fits especially well for teams on a higher-deductible medical plan or in physical lines of work where injuries are simply more likely.

How it works

  1. We review your medical planWe find where the gaps are, the deductible, the out-of-pocket max, the things a health plan alone won't cover.
  2. We check the coverage scopeWe match the plan to the work your team actually does, on the job, off the job, or both.
  3. We set up enrollmentWe add it to your online system so employees can sign up on their own time.
  4. We educate the teamWe explain how it pays, because a benefit only helps if people understand it.
  5. We support claimsWhen something happens, we help your employee file and get paid quickly.

Accident coverage pairs naturally with critical illness, hospital indemnity, and group life, so your team is covered for the gaps a medical plan tends to miss.

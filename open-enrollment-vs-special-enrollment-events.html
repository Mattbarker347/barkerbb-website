Coverage, Compared

Open enrollment vs special enrollment events.

There are exactly two times an employee can change their coverage: the yearly window, and after a qualifying life event. Knowing which is which keeps your team covered and keeps you out of compliance trouble. Here's the difference.

The yearly window, or a life-event window

Open enrollment is the set period each year when any eligible employee can enroll or change their elections. Special enrollment is a shorter window that opens only when a qualifying life event happens, like a marriage or a birth. One is scheduled, the other is triggered.

Most of the time, an employee's coverage is locked for the plan year. These two windows are the exceptions, and they work very differently.

How open enrollment works

Once a year, you set an open enrollment window, usually a week or two, when every eligible employee can join the plan, drop it, switch options, or change who's covered. It's the predictable, scheduled chance to make changes, and it's when most of the year's elections happen. We run the meeting and handle the system.

How special enrollment works

Outside that window, an employee can only make changes after a qualifying life event. The event opens a short special enrollment window, often around thirty days, to make a change that fits the event. Miss the window, and they generally wait until the next open enrollment.

What counts as a qualifying life event

Common ones are marriage or divorce, the birth or adoption of a child, and a loss of other coverage, like a spouse losing their job-based plan. Each opens a limited window tied to the change.

Why the difference matters

For you, it's about compliance and timing: changes have to fit one of these two windows, and life-event changes have deadlines. For employees, it's about not getting stuck. We track both with you so the right changes happen in the right window, all year, not just at renewal.

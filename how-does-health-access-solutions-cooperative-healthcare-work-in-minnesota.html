Benefits Questions, Answered

How does Health Access Solutions actually work?

Health Access Solutions works differently from a normal insurance plan, which is exactly why it can cost less, and why it isn't for everyone. Here's the plain-English version of how it pays and what to watch for.

A reimbursement model, not traditional insurance

Health Access Solutions is a reimbursement-based, defined-contribution healthcare solution. Instead of a traditional premium-and-network plan, it sets a per-incident deductible, the member covers that, and the rest of the incident gets reimbursed. It's built to lower the total cost of covering a team.

The mechanics are simpler than traditional insurance once you see them laid out. Here's how it pays, and the honest caveats.

How the reimbursement works

The plan comes in three options based on a per-incident deductible: $1,000, $2,500, or $4,000. When an incident happens, the member covers the deductible and Health Access Solutions reimburses the rest of that incident. There's a cap of two incidents per year, so a third incident in the same year is fully covered with no deductible. (Plan details govern the specifics for your group.)

Is it ACA-compliant?

No, not on its own. That's the key thing to understand. Because it isn't traditional ACA insurance, it can cost less, but it also isn't a one-to-one replacement for a major medical plan for every employee.

What about preexisting conditions?

Health Access Solutions carries a waiting period on preexisting conditions. So we don't leave anyone without a path, we pair it with an individual-coverage option, an ICHRA or an individual plan, for employees who need traditional coverage or have preexisting conditions. Everyone gets a route that fits.

Who it tends to fit

Employers focused on the total cost of coverage, with a team that can work with the structure. For some groups it's a clear win, and for others a traditional plan is still the better call. We run the comparison so the decision is based on real numbers, not a pitch.

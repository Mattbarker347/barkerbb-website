Benefits Questions, Answered

Do I need a broker to get group health insurance?

Short answer: no, you can go straight to a carrier. But before you do, it's worth knowing what a broker actually does and what it costs, because the answer surprises most owners.

You can go direct, but here's what you'd give up

Nothing requires you to use a broker to set up group health insurance. You can work directly with a single carrier. The catch is that you'd only see that one carrier's plans, and you'd handle everything yourself, from the paperwork to the claims calls.

A benefits broker is an independent guide who works across carriers on your behalf. Here's what that actually looks like, and why it usually doesn't cost you anything extra.

A broker shops the whole market

Instead of one carrier's plans, you see real quotes from across the market, Blue Cross Blue Shield of Minnesota, HealthPartners, Medica, UnitedHealthcare, and the regional carriers, modeled side by side so you can compare.

Using a broker doesn't raise your cost

This is the part owners don't expect. A broker's compensation is built into the plan by the carrier, so the rate you pay is the same whether you use a broker or go direct. You get the help without paying more for it.

A broker stays on all year

The carrier sells you a plan. A broker handles enrollment, sits with your employees, fields the claims questions, and starts on your renewal months ahead so you're never scrambling. Your team calls us, not your HR person, when something goes sideways.

That's the whole idea behind what we do. We take the benefits side off your plate and stay on it, and it doesn't cost you more than doing it alone would.

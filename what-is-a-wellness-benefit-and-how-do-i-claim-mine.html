Benefits Questions, Answered

What's a wellness benefit, and how do I claim mine?

It's one of the most-missed benefits out there: money you've already got coming for doing something you probably did anyway, like your yearly checkup. Here's what it is and how to actually claim it.

Money for the care you already get

A wellness benefit pays a set amount when you complete a covered routine visit or screening, often once a year per covered person. It's built into a lot of supplemental and benefit plans, and it's easy to leave on the table simply because people don't know it's there.

The idea is simple: the plan rewards preventive care. You go in for the routine visit or screening your plan covers, and the benefit pays out. Most people just forget to file for it, which is the only reason it goes unused.

What counts

It varies by plan, but it's usually a routine, preventive visit or screening, the kind of yearly checkup or test you'd get anyway. Each covered person on the plan can typically claim it once in the benefit year.

How to claim it

You've got two easy paths. You can log in to your plan's member site and submit it there, or you can send it to us through our website. Either way, you'll provide the doctor you saw, the date you went, the clinic, and any supporting documents.

We handle the filing

Once you send us those details, we take it from there and handle the filing for you. That's the whole point of having us on your side, the benefit is only worth something if it actually gets claimed, so we make sure it does.

If you're not sure whether your plan includes a wellness benefit, just ask us. It's a quick check, and it's the kind of money that's worth not leaving behind.

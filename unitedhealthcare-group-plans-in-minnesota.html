Carriers We Quote

UnitedHealthcare group plans for Minnesota businesses.

UnitedHealthcare is one of the biggest health carriers in the country, and its reach is the reason it lands on a lot of short lists. The question for your group is whether that national scale actually earns its keep, and that's what an independent broker is for. We quote it next to the other major carriers so you can judge.

What UnitedHealthcare brings to a Minnesota group

UnitedHealthcare is one of the largest health carriers in the country and part of UnitedHealth Group, which is headquartered right here in Minnetonka. For group health, its draw is network breadth, a wide national footprint that suits teams with people in more than one state.

Where UnitedHealthcare often stands out is reach. If your team is spread across state lines or you have people who travel, a broad national network means they can find in-network care almost anywhere, which is harder to match with a strictly regional plan.

We're independent, so we don't lead with any one carrier. We pull a real UnitedHealthcare quote and set it next to Blue Cross Blue Shield of Minnesota, HealthPartners, Medica, and the regional carriers, then show you where the national network helps your group and where a local carrier might serve you better.

Where UnitedHealthcare tends to fit

Broad national network

Wide coverage across state lines, a fit for teams that travel or have employees in more than one state.

Scale and tools

A large carrier with deep resources, member apps, and telehealth built into most plans.

A range of plan designs

Copay plans, high deductible options that pair with an HSA, and structures that flex to your budget.

Compared, not assumed

We quote UnitedHealthcare against the other major carriers so the choice rests on real numbers.

Benefits Questions, Answered

What's supplemental insurance, and do my employees need it?

It's the coverage that pays your employee cash when life happens, on top of their regular health plan. Whether your team needs it depends mostly on the medical plan they're already on. Here's how to tell.

Coverage that fills the gaps a medical plan leaves

Supplemental insurance pays a cash benefit directly to an employee when a covered event happens, an injury, a serious illness, a hospital stay, on top of whatever their medical plan pays. It's usually voluntary, so employees choose it and pay through payroll at group rates.

Even a strong medical plan leaves a deductible, copays, and an out-of-pocket maximum, plus all the costs that aren't medical at all. Supplemental coverage is built to land cash in the employee's hands to cover that gap.

What it includes

The common lines are accident insurance, critical illness, and hospital indemnity. Each pays a set cash benefit tied to an event, and the money goes to the employee to spend however they need, not to a provider.

Who actually benefits from it

It matters most for teams on a higher-deductible medical plan, where a single big claim could mean a large out-of-pocket hit before insurance fully kicks in. For those employees, a cash benefit can be the difference between a manageable event and a financial setback.

What it costs you

Usually little or nothing. Because supplemental is typically voluntary, employees pay the premium through payroll, so you can add the whole line at little or no direct cost to the business while giving your team real protection and group pricing.

So do your employees need it? If they're on a high-deductible plan, it's worth offering, and letting them decide. We look at where your medical coverage leaves gaps and recommend only the supplemental lines that actually address them.
